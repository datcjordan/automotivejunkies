BMW

Bayerische Motoren Werke (BMW) was founded through a series of merger and long acquisition throughout the early 1900s. BMW AG originated with three other manufacturing companies, Rapp Motorenwerke and Bayerische Flugzeugwerke (BFw) in Bavaria, and Fahrzeugfabrik Eisenach in Thuringia. The proud state of Bavaria located south eastern of Germany bordering several countries such as Liechtenstein, Austria and the Czech Republic is home to the 102 years old Bavarian automakers. Initially focus on engine manufacturing activities then soon after move into motorcycles and eventually cars. BMW has come a long way and currently owns several well-known brands; Rolls Royce and Mini. BMW history on EV can be trace back to its BMW 1602e in 1972. But the recent commitment and bandwagon is clear with the release of their BMW i3 and i3s. The model focuses on potential future mobility, which features sturdy and lighter body structure made from top grade carbon fiber materials with significantly lower center of gravity, thanks to its low-mounted batteries placement design contributing better handling experience for drivers.

ABOUT THIS CAR

Starting this November, battery capacity in every new 2019 BMW i3 and i3s increases by over 25%. That’s more than double the energy of the original i3 – which means you can enjoy every new feature, including enhanced iDrive, standard BMW Navigation, and optional WiFi and Wireless Phone Charging for even more miles. And with an exclusive new exterior color and updated upholstery options, the future of electric mobility has never looked better.
